Protecting Maui Dolphins: A Shared Responsibility

Learn about the Māui dolphin, the threats they face, and how you can help.

The Māui Dolphin's Unique Life

Protect

Māui dolphins are protected through fishing restrictions in some areas, but more coverage is needed. Avoid supporting fisheries that use gillnets in their habitat and support protected area expansion.

Additional info: The New Zealand government monitors dolphin populations and enforces regulations to minimize accidental catch. Local volunteers often participate in beach cleanups to reduce marine hazards.

Preserve

Preserving clean coastal waters helps prey species thrive. Reduce runoff and plastic pollution, and back community efforts that restore coastal habitats and water quality.

Additional info: Participating in beach cleanups or community projects can directly improve water quality and provide safe habitats for dolphins and other marine life.

Population

There are only a few dozen adult Māui dolphins left—they are one of the rarest dolphins in the world. Small population size means every individual is critical.

Additional info: Scientists track the population using photo identification and acoustic monitoring to understand threats and implement conservation strategies.

Habitats

Māui dolphins live close to shore on the west coast of North Island, NZ. They use shallow bays and coastal shelves areas vulnerable to human activity and development.

Additional info: Avoid boating in sensitive areas and report sightings to help researchers map dolphin movements and protect critical habitats.

Awareness

Public awareness drives conservation. Share accurate information, support monitoring programmes, and report sightings to local conservation groups to help track the population.

Additional info: Social media campaigns, school projects, and community talks help increase knowledge and promote protective measures.

How to Help

Donate to trusted organisations, choose sustainable seafood, reduce coastal pollution, and support policies that ban harmful fishing gear in Māui dolphin habitat.

Additional info: Volunteering, fundraising events, and advocacy for stronger marine protection laws make a real difference for dolphin survival.
